NEUROPLASTICITY AND GROWTH MINDSET

7 May 2019

Referring back to my previous post on emotional intelligence, I have to admit that I was wrong about IQ. Looking a little deeper and reading a few articles about growth mindset and neuroplasticity I realized that there is nothing static about our brains, including IQ. In fact, thinking that IQ is a static value is an example of fixed mindset approach.

Getting back to the subject of this post. What I think is important about growth and fixed mindset, is understanding that we make choices between two of them every day. Neuroplasticity is a fact. There are number of studies proving that growth mindset boosts learning experience. Why not make a choice each time when faced with a challenge than?

I was thinking of how to incorporate a growth mindset into my learning experience. There are a few tools that already facilitate it. That includes reflection, meditation, writing this sort of posts. At the end, a lot about it is self-awareness and being able to identify which state am I in, and consciously switch it if needed. Or celebrate, if it’s not. This makes it really close to emotional intelligence as an ability to identify and regulate emotions. Mindset is essentially how do we feel about certain situation. Being aware and regulating these two mindset is essentially mastering EQ.
